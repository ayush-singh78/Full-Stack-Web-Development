Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda[a] (12 January 1863 – 4 July 1902), born Narendranath Datta[b] was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, author, religious teacher, and the chief disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna.[4][5]

About the Legend

  1. Born on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India.
  2. Disciple of Sri Ramakrishna, a famous spiritual leader.
  3. Famous Speech at the Parliament of the World’s Religions in Chicago in 1893, starting with "Sisters and Brothers of America."
  4. Promoted Hinduism and taught about universal tolerance and religious harmony.
  5. Established Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 to promote education, service, and spirituality.
  6. Believed in youth empowerment and the importance of education for nation-building.
